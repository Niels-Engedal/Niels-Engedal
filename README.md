## 🧠 What I’m interested in

I’m finishing my bachelor’s in Cognitive Science and continuing into the master’s. My main interest is the space between **human behaviour**, **data**, and **useful systems**.

I’m drawn to projects where the real world is too complex, dynamic, or messy to understand by intuition alone — but where good models, interfaces, and visualizations can help people make better decisions.

Some themes I keep coming back to:

- **Human decision-making** in dynamic environments  
- **Browser-based experiments** and cognitive testing tools  
- **Spatial models** for movement, reachability, and tactical behaviour  
- **Computer vision** for practical real-world detection tasks  
- **Geospatial data systems** and recommendation engines  
- **Full-stack research tools** that people can actually use

## 🌿 The kind of problems I like

I like the point where raw reality becomes structured enough to reason about:

```text
messy behaviour → careful data → useful models → better human decisions
```

That can mean tracking movement, designing experiments, modelling spatial possibilities, building dashboards, or making small tools that help people understand something faster than they could from raw data alone.

For me, the interesting part is not replacing people with automation. It is building systems where humans and machines each do the part they are best suited for.

## 🧭 Project style

I tend to care about:
